# Content distribution flow

A repeatable sequence for publishing one piece of content across all channels without duplicating work. Built around a content pyramid: **one long-form essay generates many derivatives.**

---

## The content pyramid

```
                   ┌──────────────────────┐
                   │  1 · Substack essay  │   ← The source of truth
                   │  (1,500–2,000 words) │
                   └──────────────────────┘
                             │
                    (everything below is derived)
                             │
         ┌───────────────────┼────────────────────┐
         │                   │                    │
         ▼                   ▼                    ▼
  LinkedIn post        Twitter thread       serebrum.ai/writing
  (native, ~1200 ch)   (5–7 tweets)         (auto-pulls from RSS)
         │                   │
         ▼                   ▼
  LinkedIn DMs         Twitter replies
  (cold + warm)        (investor engagement)
         │
         ▼
  Cold email
  (personalized, post = pitch deck)
```

Write the essay once. Everything downstream either derives from it or points at it. This is how you get 3–5 weeks of distribution out of one day of writing.

---

## Publishing sequence — one essay, 14-day rollout

### Day 0 — Publish
- **Morning**: Publish on Substack (Tue or Wed 8 AM ET).
- Within 1 hour: Post native on LinkedIn (from `writing/drafts/02-linkedin-manifesto.md` or `03-linkedin-story.md`).
- Immediately: Drop Substack link as **first comment** on the LinkedIn post.
- Reply to every LinkedIn comment in first 60 min → algorithm boost.
- Your `serebrum.ai/writing` page auto-updates within 1 hour (RSS revalidation).

### Day 0+3 hrs — Twitter/X
- Post a 5–7 tweet thread summarizing the essay. Use punchiest arguments — thread should stand alone for someone who never clicks through.
- Last tweet: link to Substack.
- Pin the thread to your profile for 2 weeks.

### Day 1 — Warm-intro sweep
- Go through LinkedIn post engagement (likes + comments). Any investor, operator, or relevant person who engaged = warm lead.
- Send 3–5 warm intro requests via [04-outreach-templates.md](../drafts/04-outreach-templates.md).

### Day 2 — Cold outreach begins
- Start with 5 cold DMs and 5 cold emails (LinkedIn DMs + email). Personalize each.
- Track in a spreadsheet: name, firm, channel, date, response, next action.

### Day 3–5 — Response management
- Reply to any inbound DMs/emails within 24 hours. Never let interest go cold.
- Schedule Zooms via Calendly for anyone who asks.

### Day 7 — Second wave
- Repost the LinkedIn post as a native comment on someone else's relevant post, or reshare with a new angle.
- Send 5 more cold outreaches.
- Post a **Substack Note** (the short-form side of Substack) with one punchy takeaway + link.

### Day 10 — Follow-up
- For any cold outreaches from Day 2 that didn't respond, send the follow-up from [04-outreach-templates.md](../drafts/04-outreach-templates.md). One follow-up only.

### Day 14 — Close the loop
- Review analytics: Substack opens/reads, LinkedIn views/comments, website traffic, replies.
- Note what worked and what didn't.
- Decide what to write next based on which argument landed hardest.

## Channel matrix — what each is for

| Channel | Purpose | Format | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Substack** | Long-form thesis, email list growth | Essay, 1,500–2,000 words | Every 2–4 weeks |
| **serebrum.ai/writing** | SEO, brand authority, owned content | Auto-mirror of Substack | No work (automatic) |
| **LinkedIn post** | Distribution to investors/operators | Native text, ~1,200 chars, link in first comment | Per essay + 1–2 between essays |
| **Twitter/X thread** | Distribution to AI + VC Twitter | 5–7 tweets, standalone value | Per essay |
| **Substack Notes** | Short-form, builds Substack audience | 1–3 sentences + link | 2–3x per week |
| **LinkedIn DM** | 1:1 outreach to warm/cold investors | Personalized short message | 5–10/week during active outreach |
| **Cold email** | 1:1 to investors not active on LinkedIn | 3-paragraph personalized | 3–5/week |
| **Twitter DM** | 1:1 to AI/VC Twitter natives | Brief, casual | Ad-hoc |

## Other channels worth adding

Based on your audience (investors, operators, founders):

### Tier 1 — high ROI, low effort
- **Hacker News** — post the essay to HN Sundays (highest-engagement day for serious tech content). If it ranks even mid-page, you get thousands of relevant readers. Don't spam; one submission per essay.
- **Reddit**: `r/venturecapital`, `r/startups`, `r/artificial` — post the essay sparingly, engage in comments.
- **Indie Hackers** — friendlier audience for studio/builder content.
- **Founder slack/discord communities** — On Deck, Pioneer, accelerator alumni groups.

### Tier 2 — higher effort, compounding returns
- **Podcast guest appearances** — pitch yourself to 2–3 AI/VC podcasts. Each gives 30–60 min of your thesis to a new audience. Search "AI venture" on Spotify, make a list of 20, pitch 5.
- **Newsletter cross-promotion** — trade mentions with other AI/venture newsletter writers. Substack has a "Recommendations" feature that automates this.
- **Twitter Spaces / LinkedIn Live** — host 30-min conversations on your thesis. Builds audience faster than text alone.

### Tier 3 — paid/strategic
- **Sponsor a relevant newsletter** (e.g., The Neuron, TLDR AI, Stratechery) — direct to exactly your audience, $500–5K per placement. Only after you have a concrete ask (fundraise, hiring, pilot partner).
- **Conference panels/talks** — AI summit, Slush, SuperVenture. Expensive in time, but massive warm-intro network.
- **Editorial placement** — pitch op-eds to The Information, Axios, Insider. High-bar but huge amplification.

## Tools and systems

- **Notion or Airtable** — editorial calendar + outreach tracker
- **Buffer or Typefully** — schedule LinkedIn + Twitter posts
- **Calendly** — immediate scheduling after interest
- **Substack Notes + Substack Recommendations** — built-in audience growth
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** — network mapping for warm intros (see [04-outreach-templates.md](../drafts/04-outreach-templates.md))
- **Shortcut/Alfred/Raycast snippets** — save the cold DM and email templates as snippets you can paste with two keystrokes

---

## The fail mode to avoid

**Write once, post once, move on.** Most founders publish, wait 24 hours for engagement, then get discouraged and stop. Your first post of any piece of content reaches maybe 10% of the audience that will eventually see it. The compounding value comes from the 14-day rollout *above*, not the day of publication.

Treat publishing day as 10% of the work. The next two weeks of distribution are the other 90%.
