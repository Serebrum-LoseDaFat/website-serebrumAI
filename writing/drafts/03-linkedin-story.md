# Main post

A partner at an established VC told me: "We're struggling to figure out which AI startups to back."

Wrong question.

Pre-AI, VCs got very good at picking the best solution inside a sector they knew cold. That worked when solutions were expensive to build.

They're not expensive anymore.

Anyone can stand up a functional AI-native product in a week. Foundation models made intelligence abundant. Streams replaced batch decisions. Distribution is global by default.

So sector depth stops being the edge.

What replaces it?

Cross-industry pattern recognition. Not "which AI startup wins in healthcare" — but "which whole operating model is structurally ready to be replaced, and does the same replaceable shape show up in healthcare, finance, logistics, and product development?"

That reframes what venture investing is for. Not picking winners inside a sector. Picking systems to rebuild.

We went from "invest in industries" to "rebuild them."
