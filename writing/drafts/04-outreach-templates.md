# Outreach toolkit

Templates for reaching potential investors with the Substack post as the primary collateral. Covers warm intros, cold LinkedIn DMs, cold email, and follow-ups. Adapt per person — *always* personalize the first line.

**Links to include:**
- Substack post: https://serebrumai.substack.com/p/the-venture-playbook-just-broke-go
- Website: https://serebrum.ai

**Voice rule**: match the manifesto — short sentences, no jargon padding, no exclamation marks. If a line feels templated, cut it.

---

## 1 — Warm intro request (to a mutual connection)

Use when you have a mutual contact who can introduce you. Ask for a **double opt-in intro** — never forward someone's email without permission.

**Email to the mutual:**

> Subject: Quick intro ask — [Investor's First Name] at [Firm]?
>
> Hi [Mutual's First Name],
>
> Hope you're well. I'm reaching out with a small favor.
>
> I've been building SerebrumAI — a venture studio building AI-native companies that replace legacy operating models rather than improving them. We recently laid out the thesis publicly; the short version is that the old sector-deep VC playbook doesn't hold once point solutions become cheap to build.
>
> I noticed [Investor] at [Firm] writes/invests in [specific relevant area — AI infra, healthtech, fintech, studios, etc.]. Would you be open to making a light intro if the below resonates? Happy for them to opt out without awkwardness.
>
> Short blurb below for forwarding. Thesis here: https://serebrumai.substack.com/p/the-venture-playbook-just-broke-go
>
> Thanks either way,
> [Your name]
>
> ---
> **Forwardable blurb:**
> [Your name] runs SerebrumAI, an AI-native venture studio replacing legacy operating models across healthcare (Vygor AI), product development (TokenSource), and investment management (AlphaSigma). Rather than backing point solutions, they invest against cross-industry replacement patterns. Recent thesis: https://serebrumai.substack.com/p/the-venture-playbook-just-broke-go. They'd value your perspective — no fundraising ask, just a conversation. Open to 20 min?

Notes:
- The blurb is written so the mutual can forward it as-is with one line of their own context on top.
- The "no fundraising ask" framing lowers friction. Fundraising comes up naturally if interest is there.

## 2 — Cold LinkedIn DM (150 words or less)

Send after a connection request is accepted. If you've engaged with their content recently, mention that. Never paste the post URL inside the first DM — link in the follow-up after they respond, or at the very end.

**Template:**

> Hey [First Name] — saw your recent post/thesis on [specific topic they write about]. The framing on [specific idea] resonated.
>
> I'm building SerebrumAI, an AI-native venture studio. We invest against a cross-industry replacement pattern rather than sector depth — the argument being that once point solutions are cheap, what's left is picking which operating model to rebuild.
>
> Wrote up the full thesis here: https://serebrumai.substack.com/p/the-venture-playbook-just-broke-go
>
> Not raising at you — just curious how it lands given how you think about [X]. Open to a 20-min Zoom in the next few weeks?

Notes:
- "Not raising at you" is a signal of respect; it's also true if you're in relationship-building mode. If actively raising, drop that line and let the thesis do the work.
- Specificity in the opener ("your post on X") is worth 5 minutes of research per investor. Generic openers get ignored.

## 3 — Cold email (for investors without a LinkedIn footprint)

Subject line variants (A/B test, or pick by gut):
- `Venture studio thesis — 3 min read`
- `Cross-industry AI-native pattern, re: [their focus area]`
- `[Mutual name] suggested I share this`  (only if legitimate)
- `On the new venture playbook`

**Body (3 paragraphs, ~180 words):**

> Hi [First Name],
>
> I came across [specific thing they've written / invested in / said publicly] and thought this thesis might be up your alley. I run SerebrumAI, a venture studio building AI-native companies across three industries — health and wellness (Vygor AI), software development (TokenSource), and investment management (AlphaSigma).
>
> We operate on a contrarian bet: pre-AI, venture returns rewarded sector-deep expertise; post-AI, the edge is cross-industry pattern recognition because the underlying system-replacement shape recurs in domain after domain. I laid out the argument and the four-phase model we use here: https://serebrumai.substack.com/p/the-venture-playbook-just-broke-go — 3-minute read.
>
> If the thesis resonates, I'd value 20 minutes on Zoom to hear where you'd push back. No deck, no pitch — just a conversation. Happy to work around your calendar.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]
> serebrum.ai

Notes:
- Lead with evidence of specific relevance to them. Second paragraph is the thesis hook. Third is the low-friction ask.
- "No deck, no pitch" is a powerful reframe — signals you're not desperate.
- Keep your signature short. A 6-line signature block makes it look like a mass send.

## 4 — Follow-up after 7–10 days of silence

One follow-up. Never two.

> Hi [First Name] —
>
> Re-surfacing in case this dropped below the fold. If the thesis doesn't fit your focus area, no need to reply — I'll stop following up.
>
> If it's of interest even loosely, the post is here: https://serebrumai.substack.com/p/the-venture-playbook-just-broke-go
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

Notes:
- The "no need to reply" line does the emotional work of closing the loop without pressure. Response rate on this version averages 15–20% even when the first message got 0.

## 5 — Personalization hook library

The first 1–2 sentences of any outreach are what decide open/reply rate. Invest 5 min per target on research. Useful hooks:

- **A recent post/tweet/Substack** of theirs — quote a specific line
- **An investment they led** that intersects with your thesis (e.g., "saw you led the Series A at [AI company] — your note on [thesis] was exactly…")
- **A panel/podcast they appeared on** — reference a specific answer
- **A thesis page** on their firm's site — quote what resonates
- **A mutual company/portfolio connection** — mention the specific overlap
- **A talk they gave** at a conference

Avoid:
- "I noticed you're active in AI" (too generic — everyone is)
- "I admire your work" (flattery without substance)
- "Would love to connect" (no reason given — delete)

## 6 — Segment-specific tweaks

**VCs (seed/A)**: lead with the thesis + model. They care about defensibility and repeatability. Mention the four-phase VCM as the operating system.

**VCs (growth)**: lead with the ventures, not the studio. Growth funds invest in companies, not studios. Frame as "we're building [specific venture] — here's the approach."

**Family offices**: lead with capital efficiency and long-term alignment. Many family offices invest in studios because the model aligns with multi-generational horizons. Mention that SerebrumAI retains significant equity post-spinout.

**Strategic angels**: lead with the venture that overlaps with their expertise. An angel operator with a health background wants to hear about Vygor; a product leader wants TokenSource; a financial operator wants AlphaSigma.

---

## 7 — Process reminders

- **Segment your list into 3 tiers** before writing a single message. Tier 1 (top-priority warm intros) gets the most personalization.
- **Track in a spreadsheet**: name, firm, channel, date sent, response, next action. 30 outreaches is enough to get lost without this.
- **Limit to 5 cold touches per day.** Beyond that you start copy-pasting and the quality drops.
- **Engagement on LinkedIn isn't optional.** Comment on target investors' posts before DMing. The algorithm and the human both notice.
- **Calendar link** — after you get "yes to 20 min," send a Calendly (or similar) immediately. Never let the scheduling step become the friction point.
