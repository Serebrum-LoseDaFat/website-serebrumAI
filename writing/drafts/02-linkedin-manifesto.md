# Main post

The venture playbook just broke.

Pre-AI, investors went deep. Pick a sector. Master it. Back or build multiple solutions inside it. Field-deep specialization was the edge.

That stopped working.

Three forces changed the math:

→ Intelligence is abundant. What used to take a team of ML engineers now takes a prompt and a week.

→ Data is real-time. Sensors and streams replaced batch decisions.

→ Distribution is global by default. Regulatory labyrinths and local networks aren't the moat they used to be.

The consequence: a point solution is cheap to build now. Anyone can stand one up in a week. Sector-deep expertise compounds into a commodity.

The new edge isn't depth. It's cross-industry pattern recognition — seeing the same replaceable shape play out in healthcare, finance, software development, logistics — and knowing which whole *system* is structurally ready to be replaced.

Not which feature to improve. Which operating model to replace.

The AI bet isn't a point solution. It's industry transformation.
